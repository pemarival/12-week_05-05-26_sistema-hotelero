# Hotel system — Product need understanding

## 1. General vision

The hotel system aims to centralize the operation of a hotel or a group of hotel branches by integrating room management, booking, stay tracking, internal sales, billing, inventory, notification, security, and maintenance.

From a business perspective, the main need is to control the full hotel service cycle: from checking room availability, creating a booking, registering guest arrival, tracking product or service consumption during the stay, and closing the process through check out and billing.

The product should reduce manual work, improve payment traceability, control the real status of each room, manage dynamic pricing, and support decision-making with reliable operational information.

## 2. Customer need

The customer needs a system that allows the hotel business to operate in an organized way, with centralized information available to the different roles of the organization.

The identified needs are:

- Manage base information for customer, employee, hotel company, legal information, payment method, day type, and price.
- Administer the physical distribution of the business: branch, room, room type, and room status.
- Manage the main business process: booking, availability, check in, check out, and stay sequence.
- Register additional consumption during the stay, such as product and service.
- Control pre billing, partial payment, invoice, and purchase detail.
- Manage inventory, supplier, availability, and product or service tracking.
- Send notification related to promotion, alert, term and condition, and customer loyalty.
- Control system access through user, person, role, permission, module, and view.
- Manage room maintenance due to use, remodeling, or operational unavailability.

## 3. Parameterization module

The parameterization module represents the administrative foundation of the system. It allows the business to configure the data that supports the daily hotel operation.

This module manages customer, employee, hotel company, legal information, payment method, day type, and price. This configuration is required for the rest of the modules to work correctly.

One of the most relevant elements is dynamic price management, because the value of a room or service may change depending on season, holiday, weekday, special event, or commercial rule defined by the business.

## 4. Distribution module

The distribution module represents the physical and commercial structure of the hotel.

It manages branch, room, room type, and room status. This information is essential to check availability, assign bookings, block rooms for maintenance, and offer a clear catalog to the customer.

Room status should show whether a room is available, occupied, booked, under cleaning, blocked, or under maintenance, according to the rules defined by the business.

## 5. Service delivery module

This module is the business core. It centralizes room booking, cancellation, availability, room catalog, check in, check out, and stay sequence.

The stay sequence allows the system to register what happens while the customer occupies a room. Product sales and service sales can be associated with this sequence and later affect pre billing and invoice generation.

This module should allow the business to know at any time which customer is staying, in which room, since when, until when, what consumption has been registered, and what the current stay status is.

## 6. Billing module

The billing module consolidates the amounts generated by booking, stay, product, and service.

It should support pre billing, partial payment tracking, invoice, and purchase detail for product or service. This allows the business to manage bookings with deposits, partial payments, pending balances, and final settlement at check out.

The pre invoice acts as an anticipated view of accumulated values before issuing the final invoice.

## 7. Inventory module

The inventory module controls product and service offered by the hotel, as well as supplier, tracking, and availability.

This module is required to avoid selling unavailable products, control stock, register movements, and support replenishment.

It can also support the sale of internal hotel services, even if some services do not have physical stock like products do.

## 8. Notification module

The notification module communicates relevant information to the customer.

It can be used for promotion, alert, term and condition before purchasing product or service, and customer loyalty.

This module improves customer experience, communicates commercial rules, prevents conflicts related to purchase conditions, and strengthens the relationship after the stay.

## 9. Security module

The security module controls system access.

It should manage person, user, role, permission, module, and view. Its purpose is to ensure that each user only accesses the functionality that matches their responsibility inside the hotel.

For example, a reception employee could manage booking and check in, while an administrator could access parameterization, billing, inventory, and maintenance.

## 10. Maintenance module

The maintenance module controls the operational availability of rooms.

It should register maintenance due to use, maintenance due to remodeling, and tracking of each room status. It should also feed an availability, maintenance, and tracking dashboard.

This module is critical because a room with active maintenance should not be offered as available for booking or check in.

## 11. Business core

The system core is focused on hotel service delivery.

The main flow can be understood as follows:

1. The customer checks room catalog and availability.
2. The system calculates price according to room type, day type, and dynamic price rule.
3. The customer creates a booking.
4. The system allows partial payment registration if applicable.
5. When the customer arrives, check in is registered.
6. During the stay, product and service consumption is registered.
7. At the end, check out is registered.
8. The system generates pre invoice and final invoice.
9. The room status changes to cleaning, available, or maintenance.

## 12. Expected product outcome

The product should allow the hotel to operate in a traceable, controlled, and scalable way.

The main value for the customer is having a platform that connects real room availability with booking, payment, billing, inventory, and maintenance. This reduces operational errors and improves the final customer experience.

## 13. Pending confirmation

Pending:

- Exact rules for dynamic price calculation.
- Official statuses for booking, room, invoice, and payment.
- Real booking cancellation flow.
- Refund or cancellation penalty policy.
- Types of service offered by the hotel.
- Required notification channels.
- Real system roles.
